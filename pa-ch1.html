03. Historical Evolution

1. Ancient & Classical Periods

Egypt

Pharaohs used scribes & hierarchy (Tax, Pyramids).

China

Han Dynasty: Civil service exams. Confucian ethics.

India

Kautilya’s Arthashastra (Governance & Statecraft).

Rome/Greece

Administrative systems for roads, taxes, military.

2. Medieval Period (5th–15th C)

  • Feudalism: Decentralized. Power delegated to lords.
  • Islamic Caliphates: Diwan system, detailed records, postal services.
  • Byzantine: Maintained Roman traditions with strong bureaucracy.

3. Early Modern (16th–18th C)

Nation-States: Centralized power (Louis XIV).
Mercantilism: Economic management requiring bureaucracy.
Colonialism: Systems to govern colonies.

4. Modern Period (Birth of Discipline)

Woodrow Wilson

Father of PA (1887)

Separating politics from administration (Dichotomy). Professional study.

Max Weber

Ideal Bureaucracy

Hierarchical, rule-based, impersonal, meritocratic.

Frederick Taylor

Scientific Management

"One best way", efficiency, standardization.

Expansion (20th Century): Welfare states, Development Administration (newly independent nations), New Public Admin (Social equity - 1960s).

5. Contemporary Era (1980s–Present)

A. NPM (New Public Management)

Efficiency, Privatization, Customer-orientation. (Downsizing bureaucracy).

B. Governance

Collaboration with NGOs/Private sector. Transparency.

C. Digital & Smart

E-gov, AI, Blockchain, Smart Cities.

Global Perspectives

  • Western: Legality & Meritocracy.
  • Non-Western: Rich legacies (Asian/Islamic).
  • Intl Orgs: UN, World Bank promote reforms.

05. Bureaucracy Theories

Weberian Bureaucracy

Most efficient and rational form.

  • • Hierarchy of Authority
  • • Division of Labor
  • • Formal Rules
  • • Impersonality
  • • Merit-based Recruitment
Pros: Efficiency, Predictability Cons: Red Tape, Rigidity

Marxist Theory

Instrument of class domination (Capitalism).

  • • Alienation (Distance from people)
  • • Sustains inequality
  • • Concentrates power (like capital)

Comparison: Weber vs. Marx

Aspect Weberian Bureaucracy Marxist Theory
Viewpoint Bureaucracy is rational, efficient, and necessary. Bureaucracy is a tool of class domination and oppression.
Focus Structure, rules, hierarchy, efficiency. Power, inequality, alienation, class struggle.
Nature Neutral and merit-based system. Biased in favor of ruling elites.
Problem Inefficiency due to rigidity (red tape). Inequality, alienation, undemocratic control.
Weber saw: Best way to organize societies (Rational).
Marx saw: Oppressive instrument causing alienation.
